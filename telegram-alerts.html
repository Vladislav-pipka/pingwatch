How to Get Telegram Alerts When Your Website Goes Down

Quick answer: If you want Telegram alerts when your website goes down, use an uptime monitor that checks your URL automatically and sends a Telegram message when it stops responding. PingWatch monitors your site 24/7 and sends instant downtime and recovery alerts to Telegram.

If your website goes down and you only notice it when a user complains, you are finding out too late. Telegram alerts are one of the fastest ways to know that your site, landing page, API, or side project is offline.

Why Telegram alerts are useful

Email alerts can be slow to notice, especially if you do not keep your inbox open all day. Telegram notifications usually appear instantly on your phone and are easier to catch in real time.

That makes Telegram a great choice for solo developers, indie hackers, and small SaaS founders who want fast incident alerts without extra setup.

How website downtime alerts work

An uptime monitor sends automated requests to your website on a schedule. If the site stops responding or returns a bad status, the monitor marks it as down and sends you an alert.

When the site starts responding again, you can also receive a recovery notification so you know the problem is resolved.

What to look for in an uptime monitor

  • Fast checks and reliable alerts.
  • Recovery notifications when the site comes back.
  • Support for multiple channels like Telegram, Discord, Slack, and email.
  • Incident history, so you can review what happened later.

Can you monitor APIs too?

Yes. If your API has a public URL endpoint, you can monitor it the same way as a normal website. This is useful for SaaS apps, dashboards, webhooks, and backend services.
